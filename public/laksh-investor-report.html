What It Is

A parimutuel market for NBA player performance

Laksh is a skill-based prediction market wrapped in the familiar UX of a stock trading app. Every participant starts with $10,000 in virtual cash and uses it to buy and sell fractional shares in NBA players. Prices move continuously based on two forces: trading activity and live game statistics.

At the end of the NBA season, the collective prize pool — made up of all participants' deposits minus the 5% platform rake — is distributed proportionally. If your portfolio is worth 3% of all portfolios combined, you receive 3% of the prize pool. Outperform the field, walk away with more than you put in.

Laksh is not gambling. The outcome depends entirely on skill: your ability to identify undervalued players, anticipate breakout performances, and manage a portfolio better than other participants. No house edge beyond the transparent 5% rake. Every dollar in the pool was put in by a real participant.

How It Works

1

Enter the pool

Users join via a waitlist and are approved for beta access. Upon approval they receive $10,000 in virtual cash. 5% is taken upfront as platform rake — the remaining $9,500 per user enters the shared prize pool that pays out at season end.

2

Trade player shares

Users buy and sell fractional shares in any of 108 active NBA players. Every buy pushes a player's price up; every sell pushes it down. The more you trade, the larger the price impact. There is no counterparty — trades execute against an automated market maker (AMM).

3

Watch prices move with games

During NBA games, the platform polls the BallDontLie API every minute for live box scores. Each new stat event — a point scored, an assist, a steal — immediately bumps the player's price. A 30-point, 10-assist, 10-rebound game moves a player's price approximately +3.8%. After games end, prices drift back toward each player's expected fair value.

4

Build the strongest portfolio

Your portfolio value is your cash balance plus the mark-to-market value of all your shares. The goal is to grow your portfolio relative to every other participant. Picking players who outperform their priced-in expectations is the alpha edge.

5

Settle at season end

When the NBA regular season concludes, the prize pool is distributed proportionally based on each participant's final portfolio value relative to the total of all portfolios. Users can also exit early at any time for a 3% fee — that fee stays in the pool, increasing payouts for remaining participants.

Payout Structure

Proportional distribution — transparent and zero-sum

The prize pool and payout formula are fully deterministic:

Prize Pool = Σ(all deposits) × 0.95
Your Payout = (Your Portfolio Value ÷ Σ All Portfolio Values) × Prize Pool

A participant whose portfolio outperforms the field receives a payout larger than their initial deposit. A participant who underperforms receives less. The sum of all payouts equals exactly the prize pool — no money is created or destroyed.

Example — 10 users

10 users × $10,000 = $100,000 total deposits.
Prize pool = $95,000 (after 5% rake).
User with 12% portfolio share receives $11,400 — a $1,400 gain on their $10,000 entry.

Early exit

Users who withdraw before season end receive their current portfolio value minus a 3% fee. The fee is added to the prize pool, increasing payouts for everyone who stays — rewarding conviction and long-term thinking.

Pricing Engine

Three-layer blended pricing — AMM + fair value + TWAP

Player prices are not arbitrary — they are continuously computed by a pricing engine that blends three signals on every tick (every ~5 seconds):

Signal Weight What it captures
AMM spot price 15% Immediate supply/demand from buy/sell activity
Fair value (FV) 65% Model estimate from season stats: PPG, APG, RPG, efficiency
TWAP (30-min) 20% Time-weighted average — dampens manipulation and noise

The dominant weight on fair value means prices are anchored to actual player performance. A great week of games raises a player's FV and pulls their market price upward over time — regardless of whether anyone is actively trading them.

Engagement Mechanics

Real games, real stakes

Every NBA game is a live event for Laksh users. Price action during games drives emotional engagement and creates natural watch-along behavior — users open the app during games to see their portfolio move in real time.

Skill-based alpha

Unlike fantasy sports with a fixed scoring system, Laksh rewards market insight: buying undervalued players before breakouts, timing exits before slumps, and reading injury news faster than other participants.

Season-long tension

The proportional payout structure means your rank relative to others matters as much as your absolute returns. The live leaderboard and pool share percentage create persistent competitive tension over the full season.

Early exit optionality

Users are never locked in. The 3% early exit fee is low enough to not feel punitive, while being high enough to reward patience — users who stay benefit from the fees paid by those who leave.

Technology

Production-grade infrastructure, built to scale

Laksh is built on a modern, serverless stack designed for high availability and low operational overhead. The platform handles real-time price computation, live stat ingestion, and user portfolio tracking entirely through managed infrastructure — no dedicated servers to maintain.

Frontend

Next.js 15 app router, deployed on Vercel. Mobile-first PWA with real-time price polling every 5 seconds. Recharts area charts with LTTB downsampling and bidirectional EMA smoothing for professional-quality price graphs.

Backend

Supabase (PostgreSQL) for all persistent state — players, price history, positions, trades, users. Row-level security on all user data. Serverless API routes on Vercel Edge.

Live data

BallDontLie All-Star API tier for live NBA box scores. Cron fires every minute, detects live games via schedule cache, fetches stat deltas, and applies price bumps within ~60 seconds of a stat being recorded.

Automation

Three Vercel crons: price tick (every minute), stat ingest (every minute, game-aware), and daily full player stats rebuild (6am UTC). Price history auto-prunes to a rolling 7-day window. No manual operations required during the season.

Business Model

Simple, transparent revenue

Laksh earns revenue from three sources, all fully disclosed to users before they enter:

Revenue source Rate When collected
Platform rake 5% of deposit At the time of entry — deducted from prize pool
Transaction fee 0.2% base, up to 5% cap On every buy and sell — deducted from the trade amount
Early exit fee 3% of portfolio value When a user withdraws before season end — added back to prize pool

The transaction fee is dynamic: it starts at a 0.2% base and scales upward when a player's price is far from its model fair value or when volatility is elevated — discouraging speculative runs and rewarding trades that move prices toward fair value. The fee is capped at 5% per trade. Users see the exact fee amount before confirming any trade.

Note: the early exit fee is redistributed to remaining participants rather than retained as platform revenue in the current model — it functions as a retention incentive. Future versions may split this fee between the platform and the remaining pool.

Unit economics at scale: 1,000 users × $10,000 entry = $10M in deposits. At a 5% rake, platform revenue is $500,000 per season — before any early exit fee optimization. The model scales linearly with user count and requires no incremental infrastructure spend at this level.
